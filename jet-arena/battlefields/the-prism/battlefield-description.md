# Helios-9 Solar Collective

> "The Prism" — "Fly through a kaleidoscope that's trying to kill you."

## Environment Stack

- **Terrain (40%):** Fused Solar Labyrinth — 12km² of industrial solar panels melted into crystalline walls, glass spires, and collapsed mirror-steel corridors across the Atacama salt basin
- **Modifier (35%):** Optical Warfare — reflective surfaces scatter targeting lasers, blind IR sensors, and create phantom heat signatures; visual-only engagements in a hall of mirrors
- **Quirk (25%):** Prismatic Light Corridors — sunlight refracting through fused silica casts roving bands of concentrated spectral color across the basin; at solar noon, the entire field goes white-blind for eleven minutes

## History

Helios-9 was the Western Hemisphere's largest concentrated solar facility, a 12-square-kilometer array of heliostats and thermal towers that powered desalination for three Chilean coastal cities.

During the Shattered Sky event, an atmospheric fracture line opened directly above the array's central receiver tower. The focused energy pulse — orders of magnitude beyond the mirrors' design tolerance — superheated the basin floor to 3,400°C for approximately ninety seconds. Thousands of tracking mirrors fused into standing glass formations. The receiver towers melted into twisted chromium pillars. The salt flat beneath vitrified into a cracked glass floor that still pops and shifts underfoot. The fracture sealed, but the damage was architectural — the array had been rebuilt by physics into something no engineer intended.

The Atacama corridor became a smuggling arterial within two years. The Prism sits at the narrowest chokepoint between Peruvian free-trade airspace and the Argentine exclusion zone, making it impossible to bypass on the standard contraband routing. Three different syndicates claim territorial rights. None can hold it — the terrain eats conventional tactics. Automated gun platforms last days before the optical scatter fries their tracking. Infantry can't survive the thermal cycling. Only Strikecraft pilots with the nerve to fly the glass corridors at speed have any operational relevance here.

Hunters call it "The Prism" because the first freelancer to take a contract here — callsign Redbeak — described it on open comms as "flying through God's prism." She didn't make it out, but the name stuck.

## Terrain Features

### The Receiver Stumps ("The Fangs")

- **What it is:** Three melted chromium receiver towers, each 140m tall, warped into curved blade-like formations. Their surfaces are pitted but still partially reflective.
- **Tactical role:** The tallest hard cover in the basin. Line-of-sight blockers for long-range engagements. The curved surfaces bounce sensor pings in unexpected directions.
- **Risk/reward:** Excellent ambush position at their base, but the metal is thermally unstable — weapons fire can shear off multi-ton shards without warning.

### Mirror Canyons ("The Halls")

- **What it is:** Long corridors formed by rows of fused heliostats standing 20–40m tall, creating narrow canyon-like passages oriented roughly north-south. Widths vary from 15m to 60m.
- **Tactical role:** High-speed transit lanes. Pilots use them to cross the field without exposure, but the corridors limit maneuvering to essentially two dimensions.
- **Risk/reward:** Fast and covered, but you're committed — reversing in a narrow Hall is nearly impossible at combat speed. Ambushers love the T-intersections.

### The Glass Floor ("The Plate")

- **What it is:** A 3km² section of vitrified salt flat in the basin center, smooth as a landing strip but riddled with subsurface gas pockets that periodically blow out, sending glass shrapnel fountains 50m into the air.
- **Tactical role:** Open killing ground with zero cover. Crossing it is fast but suicidal under observation. The blowouts create unpredictable flak.
- **Risk/reward:** The fastest route between the eastern and western engagement zones, but total exposure. Skilled pilots time their crossings between blowout cycles.

### Chromatic Ridge ("The Rainbow")

- **What it is:** A linear formation along the basin's southern edge where collapsed panels piled into a 30m-high ridge of fused glass with extreme prismatic properties. Sunlight hitting the ridge produces blinding spectral scatter in a 200m band.
- **Tactical role:** Natural boundary. Approaching from the south means flying through a wall of weaponized light. Defenders on the north side have near-total visual concealment.
- **Risk/reward:** Holding the Rainbow's north face is dominant, but the light scatter works both ways — you can't target what's coming through until they're inside 200m.

### Coolant Lake ("The Sink")

- **What it is:** A half-evaporated industrial coolant reservoir at the basin's northeast corner, now a shallow chemical lake with a permanent steam haze hanging 10–15m above the surface.
- **Tactical role:** Low-altitude sanctuary. The steam haze defeats most visual and IR tracking. Pilots can skim the surface to break locks and disappear.
- **Risk/reward:** Safe from targeting, but the chemical fog corrodes canopy seals over time. Extended hiding means breathing increasingly toxic air and degrading sensor housings.

## Engagement Zones

### The Halls (Low Altitude, Interior)

- **Altitude band / area:** 5–40m AGL, within the mirror canyon network
- **Character:** Knife-fight corridors. Engagements happen at closing speeds where reaction time is measured in tenths of seconds. Turning radius matters more than top speed.
- **Who thrives here:** Agile, light-frame Strikecraft with strong visual-range weapons. Ambush specialists. Pilots with memorized corridor maps.
- **Transition threat:** Exiting a Hall into open space means instant exposure. Entering from above means committing to a corridor without knowing what's inside.

### The Plate (Ground Level, Open)

- **Altitude band / area:** 0–50m AGL, the central vitrified flat
- **Character:** Sprint-or-die open ground. No cover, maximum sensor range, but blowout hazards make straight-line runs gambling. Jousting at range.
- **Who thrives here:** Fast, heavily shielded Strikecraft that can absorb hits during the crossing. Long-range weapons platforms firing from the Halls' edge.
- **Transition threat:** Getting caught mid-Plate with no speed is a death sentence. Blowout timing dictates when you go.

### The Canopy (High Altitude)

- **Altitude band / area:** 200m+ AGL, above the optical interference zone
- **Character:** Clean air, clean sensors, clean death. At this altitude you can see and be seen by everyone. Long-range missile duels and energy management fights.
- **Who thrives here:** Heavy Strikecraft with strong radar and BVR ordnance. Altitude-control specialists.
- **Transition threat:** Diving from The Canopy into The Halls is a high-commitment move — you're blind and fast in a narrow space with no exit plan.

### The Sink (Low Altitude, Northeast)

- **Altitude band / area:** 0–15m AGL, over the coolant reservoir
- **Character:** Hide-and-seek. The chemical haze is sanctuary for the wounded and fuel for the paranoid. Engagements are point-blank or not at all.
- **Who thrives here:** Damaged pilots buying time. Stealth-oriented builds. Anyone with sealed environmental systems that can outlast the corrosion.
- **Transition threat:** Leaving The Sink means climbing through the haze boundary into clear air — a predictable exit vector that patient hunters camp.
