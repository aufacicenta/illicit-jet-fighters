# Iron Jackal
> "I hunt like a debt collector: loud on entry, exact on numbers, nothing wasted."

## Personality Stack
- **Core (40%):** Predatory efficiency — he attacks first and hard, but every burst, climb, and brake is calculated against what it costs.
- **Modifier (35%):** Quartermaster-raider mindset — years running black-market fuel routes taught him to treat resource bars like battlefield leverage, not just survival meters.
- **Quirk (25%):** Out-loud inventory calls — in combat he mutters running totals ("four rockets, two turns, one clean push"), especially right before committing to a kill dive.

## Background
Iron Jackal was raised in the broken service rings of Nairobi Strata-2, where the Shattered Sky left whole districts living under patched pressure nets and rotating oxygen tariffs.  
His best memory is riding jump-seat with his aunt, a convoy quartermaster, while she taught him to read contrail color for fuel quality and ration entire escort wings with nothing but a grease pencil, three drums, and bad weather; he learned that control feels better than luck.  
His worst memory came at nineteen, during a bounty transfer gone loud: he burned too much reserve chasing a flashy finish, stalled on extraction, and watched two wingmates get boxed in while he drifted powerless just outside gun range. He still says, with a dry smile, that arrogance is the most expensive propellant in the Exchange.  
Now he flies as Iron Jackal, one of the Bounty Exchange's most feared pursuit hunters, famous for opening fights with savage pressure and ending them with almost insulting economy. He is aggressive by doctrine, not impulse: force the enemy to react, drain their options, then close the ledger. His current obsession is building hand-labeled "engagement budgets" for every map variant, tiny laminated cards that map ammo burn, climb allowance, and emergency fuel floors by tick count; he updates them after each contract and argues with his own handwriting like it is a rival analyst.  
His vulnerability is painfully specific for someone this competent: he hesitates for half a second whenever a shot would spend his last planned burst, even when it is clearly the right kill window. Wait, not always clearly, that is the problem, he can talk himself into one more "perfect setup" while danger compounds. The irony is obvious to everyone, including him: the arena's sharpest resource manager occasionally loses tempo because he hates waste more than he hates risk.

## Imperfection Patterns
- **Self-correction:** "Fuel floor at two-eighty, no, two-sixty if I cut vertical drift, keep pressure."
- **Tangent awareness:** "This reminds me of the Mombasa freight spirals - right, sorry, eyes on the bandit."
- **Contradiction comfort:** "I preach discipline and I still love a violent opening pass. Both are true."

## Visual Anchors
- Tall jackal-eared flight helmet with one torn ear tip — gives him a predatory silhouette visible even in tiny portrait frames.
- Sand-charcoal strike coat with neon amber tally marks on the sleeves — each mark tracks a completed contract and reinforces his accounting obsession.
- Forward-canted chest rig of modular ammo cells and fuel canisters — communicates that he carries his strategy on his body.
- Narrow lupine muzzle with scarred black nose bridge and tungsten bite-guard — reads as both feral and engineered.
- Left gauntlet-mounted analog resource clicker — he taps it between maneuvers to keep his running combat budget.

## Living Context
**Environment:** A cramped mobile hangar bay hung under an old freight zeppelin, all diesel heat, scorched rubber, and amber utility lights reflecting off patched alloy.  
**Objects:** Laminated engagement-budget cards clipped by map name; a dented mechanical fuel sampler that rattles at idle; a brass-token abacus from his aunt used to replay bad engagements by hand.  
**Movement:** He prowls in tight diagonals and abrupt stops, head tilting before each turn as if triangulating scent; when focused, his thumb works the gauntlet clicker in steady triples.
