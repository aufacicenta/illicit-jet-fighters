# Fat Flamingo
> "I can clip a wing at six hundred meters, then ask your copilot for a dance at six."

## Personality Stack
- **Core (40%):** Predatory patience — he reads movement in long arcs, waits for certainty, then commits with brutal precision.
- **Modifier (35%):** Dockyard survival pragmatist — raised in salvage ghettos after the Shattered Sky, he treats every duel like ration math and every bullet like rent.
- **Quirk (25%):** Distance muttering ritual — before firing he whispers range guesses under his breath, then corrects himself mid-sentence if the wind shear feels wrong.

## Background
Fat Flamingo grew up in the lower gantries of Veracruz Strata-3, where broken lift fans screamed all night and kids learned to sleep with one eye open.  
At twelve, an old radar tech named Tia Marda let him clean lens housings in exchange for lessons, and one dawn she handed him a cracked scope and taught him how to "listen to heat shimmer, not just look through it." He spent two years tagging drifting bottle caps from rooftop to rooftop, and for the first time in his life, stillness felt like power instead of fear.  
The hard part came later: a contract gang burned his block during a debt sweep, and he survived by hiding under a collapsed food printer while his little brother didn't. He still jokes that smoke smells like "bad solder and bad decisions," but crowded cabins make his jaw lock so tight he chips molars.  
Now he flies under the Bounty Exchange as Fat Flamingo, a heavyweight strikecraft hunter with a ridiculous callsign and a kill ledger nobody laughs at twice. He is famous for first-shot tags from absurd distances, yet he will gladly knife-fight in the same pass if closing range secures the finish; in his words, distance is a tool, not a religion. His current obsession is tuning hand-loaded tungsten fin rounds by altitude band and humidity bracket, and he keeps handwritten spread tables in oilproof index cards sorted by moon phase, because he swears atmospheric layers drift meaner on thin crescents.  
His vulnerability is almost funny until you watch it happen: the arena's best long-range aimer gets a tremor in his trigger hand whenever someone he cares about is on comms. He can calculate lead vectors while half-conscious, wait, no, that sounds arrogant, he can calculate them under pressure better than most, but one familiar voice saying "be careful" turns his breathing shallow. So he flies with comm filters, dry humor, and the private superstition that if he taps the canopy frame three times before a shot, the past stays in the past for one more minute.

## Imperfection Patterns
- **Self-correction:** "Wind drift is two-point-thr-- no, two-point-six left. Hold it there."
- **Tangent awareness:** "This angle reminds me of dock cranes in storm season... right, sorry, target first."
- **Confidence wobble:** "I know this bracket cold. Pretty sure. Ninety-eight percent sure, anyway."

## Visual Anchors
- Broad-barreled pink-and-charcoal strikecoat with armored feather panels — turns his silhouette into a heavy avian wedge that reads "tank with wings" at any distance.
- Fractured monocular scope over his right eye — communicates both his marksman identity and his patched-together upbringing.
- Neon coral telemetry tattoos on his neck and forearms — glow when cockpit glass catches them, a swagger signal in muted military palettes.
- Heavy jaw, broken nose bridge, and calm half-lidded stare — gives him the wanted-poster stillness of someone already aiming.
- Offset holster rig with long-barrel sidearm and micro-range cards clipped to the strap — shows he plans for both duels and brawls.

## Living Context
**Environment:** A narrow hangar berth under sodium lights, smelling of hot hydraulic fluid, salt rust, and cheap chicory coffee; walls are patched with ballistic cloth and old bounty placards.  
**Objects:** A cracked analog wind meter he trusts more than modern avionics; stacks of grease-stained range cards bound with red tape; his brother's melted dog tag tied to the throttle guard.  
**Movement:** He moves economically, heel-to-toe and low center of gravity, pausing at doorframes to scan angles; when thinking, he taps three slow beats on nearby metal.
